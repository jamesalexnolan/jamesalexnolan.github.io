Accordion Registration Page

For this build, on both the Ireland and UK Nutricia sites I was tasked with amending the current registration. Turning it from a step-by-step process, into an accordion layout. Which better allows the user to navigate between each step, combine steps for quicker completetion and shows a progress bar at the top, for a better indication of time needed to complete the registration process.

Placing a review header a the top of the page, which when press, would navigate the user down to the review accordion, where they can view the tops reviews for that product.

Rotating Banners

This was a straight forward build, where a sticky banner was added to the top of the page, with three CTA's on it - Which would rotate one after the other to try and encourage the user to register with the site. This was implemented across both the Apta Club and Cow and Gate, Ireland and UK sites.

HotJar survery functionality

On both the UK Aptaclub and Cow&Gate sites, across various product pages, we ran a HotJar Survery, to gain a better understanding of customer attribution. HotJar surverys are quite simplistic, and we only wanted the survery to show when a user clicked on a 'Buy Now' CTA - To address I built out the functionality to prevent the defualt re-direct, capture the href, show the survery, and then once the user closed the survery, redirect them to the retailer they chose originally.

Navigation Menu Update

Sticky CTA

Newsletter pop-up

For this build, I replaced the newsletter sign up section in the footer, with a pop-up, to try and drive newsletter registrations. This generated a cookie, which would mean that once shown to a user, they wouldn't have to see the pop up again, prevented scrolling while the pop-up was shown, and ensured that the pop-up still linked to the database where the user information could be stored.

Virgin.

Product selection

This was one of my most challenging builds. Using completely dynamic content, I had to add in a host of functionality into the product purchase section of the Virgin Media website. Turning a step-by-step process, into a more seamless accordion layout. Elements of the accordion would change based on the users selection on the previous page - This included the accordion title - which had to reflect whether that option would be avabilabe to them, and if they'd added something as of yet or not. The accordion options that would be made visible - if they weren't in the bundle they selected, they would have to be removed, the accordion buttons on the side and also the price at the bottom of the accordions - Which was made by looping over each selected option a user had interacted with, and totaling that up.
